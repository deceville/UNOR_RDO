About Research Office

Since research precedes development, the quality of the research academe undertakes generates the fund of the knowledge upon which the quality of education depends on a large extent. In the midst of academy research endeavors are the faculty and the academic personel, the benchmark of the educational exellence, whose outputs determine the quality of education the University offers to its students (RPDO Policies and Guidelines 2010).

The academe has traditionally been the primary source of the world's expansion of knowledge and innovations. It promotes research excellence for technological, social, economic, political, and moral advancement anchored on evidence-based findings. Accordingly, research has become the lifeblood of the market of ideas and the impetus for development among the people in the academe and other fields.

Since research precedes development, the quality of research the academe undertakes generates the fund of knowledge upon which the quality of education depends on a large extent. In the midst of academic research endeavors are the faculty and the academic personnel, the benchmark of educational excellence, whose outputs determine the quality of education the University offers to its students (RPDO Policies and Guidelines, 2010). Hence, the necessity for the UNO-R community to "create scholarly leaders of science who extend the frontiers of knowledge through experimentation and verification bringing about a deeper evaluation of problems that will make them see the synthesis of faith, reason, culture, and life" (Faculty Manual, 2006, p. 4; UNO-R Student Manual, 2014).

Cognizant of these considerations and challenges as “an Augustinian Recollect Center of Learning which makes knowledge (Scientia) a structure of wisdom that leads one to the exercises of love (Caritas),” (Memorandum No. 123 Series SY 2015-2016), the Research Office (formerly known as Research, Planning and Development Office [RPDO]) of the University of Negros Occidental-Recoletos (UNO-R) was organized to produce research outputs whose quality meet the international research standards by institutionalizing research activities that promote and develop the culture of research excellence in collaboration with external organizations and other research institutions.

The Research Office (RDO) commits itself to the generation and propagation of new knowledge for human development, social sustainability, environmental protection, technological innovationm, nation building, and social transformation. It likewise commits itself to the actualization of UNO-R's commitment to the advancement of research and development, fully supportive of and complementary to teaching and community services and rersponsive to contemporary issues and problems of the University and of the society (RPDO Policies and Guidelines, 2010).

The Research Office fulfills UNO-R's aspiration to be “A Catholic University committed to the integral formation of the human person with passion for excellence and service to the Church and society," (Memorandum No. 123, Series SY 2015-2016). Specifically, the RO envisions UNO-R to attain a national and international position in education and research through the constant formation and development of scholarly leaders whose works parallel or even exceed the international standard of quality in their fields and disciplines. It likewise pursues enrichment of existing academic programs and broadening of knowledge through functional ethical research for the authentic good of the individual and the society.

The research undertakings of UNO-R deal with institutional concerns and community responsiveness. The first component intends to promote internal growth and seeks to address the University's needs.

Through institutional development, the RO aims to determine the current policies and practices of the University as well as its weaknesses and formulate appropriate solutions and policies to address institutional problems. Research under this component is the lifeblood of the University, upholding efficiency and excellence.

The second component, community responsiveness, intends to disseminate and apply knowledge beyond the University. It seeks to respond to the bigger and emerging issues on the environment, technology, community development, security, culture and the arts, and issues of national and global concern, thus performing its role in nation building and social transformation. In this context, research becomes the ray of light that reflects the University's academic excellence by generating and sharing knowledge.

The selection of priority areas of the research agenda was guided by the National Higher Education Research Agenda 2 of the Commission on Higher Education, the priority research areas of the Department of Science and Technology, the Millennium Development Goals of the United Nations Development Programme, and the Middle Term National Development Plan of the National Economic and Development Authority.

In addition, it incorporated the inputs of religious and lay administrators, the Research Core Group’s analysis of the present state of the university, and the suggestions of the Internal Research Committee composed of the research coordinators from the different colleges and units.

The RO is tasked to:

1. define the research thrust and agenda of the University, taking into consideration the priority areas outlined by the Commission on Higher Education (CHED) and other government agencies (DOST, NEDA, UNDP, etc);

2. organize and conduct research seminars/workshops and peer mentoring activities among the members of the UNO-R community to promote research awareness and encourage engagement in significant research;

3. review research practices in the University, benchmark best practices with reputable research institutions and organizations, and continuously calibrate the University research standards through recommendation of better policies for implementation (i.e. research grants and incentive schemes, research quality assurance policies, etc.);

4. implement the University faculty research program, serve as a clearing house of proposed research projects, prepare Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) for approval of research grants, monitor the conduct of the approved research projects, and publish research outputs in reputable refereed journals;

5. develop an institutional system for student research, set institutional standards, and monitor all college and department research activities;

6. disseminate the relevant research findings of both students and faculty members to the members of the academe through research presentations (i.e. research exposition/colloquium), periodic publication of an institutional journal, and utilization of research outputs through translational research activities or knowledge and technology transfer in the University or the community;

7. protect the intellectual property rights of the researchers;

8. conduct institutional research studies requested by the administration, and guide and assist service units and colleges in necessary research studies concerning their specific areas;

9. establish linkage with government and private agencies and research centers or institutions for possible sources of funds for research projects;

10. perform other research-related activities in collaboration with other institutions and/or external agencies;

11. administer Faculty, Department Heads, and Deans performance evaluations; and,

12. administer evaluation of institutional and unit programs and activities.
